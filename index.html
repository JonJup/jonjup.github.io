About

Bridging data and ecology

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Helsinki, where I hold a DFG Walter Benjamin Fellowship. My work sits at the intersection of freshwater ecology, biostatistics, and environmental data science.

My current project, PULSE, is a 24-month initiative developing novel freshwater ecotypologies for European rivers. By compiling pan-European biodiversity databases spanning over 400,000 samples across four major taxonomic groups, I aim to reshape how we classify, assess, and protect river ecosystems.

Before Helsinki, I worked in Germany where I built expertise in diatom-based biomonitoring, spatial analysis, and the development of open-source tools for ecological research.
